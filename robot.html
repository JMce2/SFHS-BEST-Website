Driving

  The design generated for driving was to use gears or wheels. This way we could attach a motor to a wooden gear, and it would directly turn the wheels. Some of the benefits of this design are that it increases torque, and drives four wheels with two motors. It is also more stable and allows for more precise turning. However, because two wheels are on the same axle, it may make turning more difficult.

Manny and Chemical Drums

  The first design for to score Manny and the chemical drums was to have a scoop on the front of the robot. We would have a stick protrude from the robot to knock over the cans, then scoop them up to place them in the back. The scoop would also be able to scoop Manny up and place him in the starting zone to be scored. Some of the benefits are that it was simple to make, it has the ability to score both objects, it is easy to control, specific aim is not required, and is stable when being carried. Some of the drawbacks are the cans have to be knocked over, it weighs a lot, you have to use a wall to get objects into the scoop, the cans could possibly fall into the robot, and needs to be shaken down after the game starts. It does require a motor, but the motor can also be used to aim the slingshot. The mechanism would also counterweight the slingshot.

Shooting the Flames

  There was one main idea of how to shoot the flames, using a slingshot. It has a metal plate that the ball leans against, and is pulled back with bungee. Then the ball is released, and a new ball falls into place. We originally used a grenade pin to release the ball, but it required too much force to release the ball, took a long time to reload, was hard to control, and required two motors. Eventually, the release mechanism was built into the firing mechanisms, making the slingshot more simple.
